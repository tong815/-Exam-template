## Category Definitions (for Teachers)

### Knowledge & Understanding (K)

Students demonstrate knowledge and understanding of:

- Mathematical terminology, notation, and definitions (e.g., function, domain, range, inverse)
- Standard procedures (factoring, solving, graphing, simplifying expressions)
- Algebraic accuracy and computational fluency
- Properties of functions (linear, quadratic, exponential, trigonometric as applicable)

**Look for:** Correct formulas, accurate substitution, proper use of symbols, recall of key facts.

---

### Thinking (T)

Students demonstrate mathematical thinking by:

- Selecting appropriate strategies for unfamiliar or multi-step problems
- Reasoning through connections between representations (graph ↔ table ↔ equation)
- Making and testing conjectures; identifying patterns
- Problem solving — breaking problems into manageable steps

**Look for:** Logical plan, justified steps, effective strategy, error detection and correction.

---

### Communication (C)

Students communicate mathematical thinking by:

- Organizing work clearly and sequentially
- Using correct mathematical notation and units
- Explaining reasoning in words where required
- Labelling graphs, axes, and key features

**Look for:** Readable solutions, complete sentences in Part C, labelled diagrams, stated restrictions.

---

### Application (A)

Students apply mathematical knowledge by:

- Modelling real-world situations with functions
- Interpreting solutions in context (units, reasonableness, limitations)
- Transferring skills to new contexts not identical to class examples
- Analysing data or scenarios to make informed decisions

**Look for:** Contextual setup, meaningful interpretation, appropriate model choice, realistic conclusions.

## Level Descriptors (1–4 Scale)

*Adapt to your board’s reporting scale (e.g., Level 1–4, or percentage bands).*

### Knowledge & Understanding (K)

| Level | Descriptor |
|:------|:-----------|
| **4** | Demonstrates thorough knowledge of content; procedures are consistently accurate |
| **3** | Demonstrates considerable knowledge; minor procedural errors |
| **2** | Demonstrates some knowledge; frequent procedural gaps |
| **1** | Demonstrates limited knowledge; major inaccuracies |

### Thinking (T)

| Level | Descriptor |
|:------|:-----------|
| **4** | Selects and applies effective strategies; reasoning is insightful and complete |
| **3** | Mostly effective strategies; reasoning generally sound |
| **2** | Inconsistent strategies; partial reasoning |
| **1** | Limited or inappropriate strategies; little evidence of reasoning |

### Communication (C)

| Level | Descriptor |
|:------|:-----------|
| **4** | Communication is clear, precise, and uses correct notation throughout |
| **3** | Communication is mostly clear with minor notation issues |
| **2** | Communication is unclear or incomplete in places |
| **1** | Communication is difficult to follow; notation often incorrect |

### Application (A)

| Level | Descriptor |
|:------|:-----------|
| **4** | Applies concepts skillfully to complex, unfamiliar, or real-world contexts |
| **3** | Applies concepts correctly in familiar and some new contexts |
| **2** | Applies concepts with limited success; weak interpretation |
| **1** | Rarely applies concepts appropriately; no meaningful context |

## Holistic Judgment Notes

When assigning an **overall achievement level** for the unit or report card:

1. **Weight Part D heavily** for Application and Thinking.
2. **Weight Part C heavily** for Communication.
3. Do not let Part A alone determine the overall level — it measures recall, not full achievement.
4. Consider **most consistent level** or **most recent evidence** per school policy.

## Sample Feedback Phrases (English)

Use on returned papers or report cards:

- **K:** “You show strong mastery of core procedures.” / “Review [topic] definitions and practice standard algorithms.”
- **T:** “Your problem-solving approach is systematic.” / “Try outlining a strategy before computing.”
- **C:** “Explanations are clear and well organized.” / “Include labelled diagrams and state domain restrictions.”
- **A:** “You connect mathematics to real situations effectively.” / “Practice interpreting answers in context with units.”
